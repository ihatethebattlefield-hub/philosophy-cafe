Siddhartha Gautama (The Buddha)

c. 563 – 483 BCE · India & Nepal
Buddhism
Biography

Siddhartha Gautama was born a prince in the Shakya clan near the Himalayan foothills. Sheltered from suffering in his youth, he encountered old age, sickness, and death and renounced his palace to seek liberation from the bondage of existence.

After years of ascetic practice and meditation, he attained enlightenment beneath a Bodhi tree and became the Buddha — the Awakened One. For the next forty-five years he wandered northern India, teaching monks, nuns, laypeople, and kings.

He founded a monastic order and left no written teachings; his discourses were preserved by memory and later committed to canonical collections. His insights into suffering, impermanence, and interdependence remain among the most influential philosophical and spiritual teachings in history.

Great Ideas
  • The Four Noble Truths: Life involves suffering; suffering has a cause; the cause can be ended; the Eightfold Path leads to that end.
  • The Middle Way: Liberation lies between the extremes of indulgence and severe asceticism — a balanced practice of ethics, meditation, and wisdom.
  • Anicca (Impermanence): All conditioned phenomena arise and pass away; clinging to what cannot last is the root of suffering.
  • Anatta (Not-Self): There is no permanent, unchanging soul; what we call self is a changing process of aggregates.
  • Dependent Origination: All things arise in dependence upon conditions; nothing exists in complete isolation.
In His Words

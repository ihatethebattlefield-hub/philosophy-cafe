Friedrich Nietzsche

1844 – 1900 · Röcken
Existentialism & Critique
Biography

A brilliant philologist who turned against his own discipline, Nietzsche wrote in aphorisms, parables, and furious poetry. He spent his most productive years in lonely Alpine sanatoriums, tormented by illness, and collapsed into madness in 1889, spending his last years under the care of his mother and sister.

He is often misread as a nihilist; in truth he diagnosed nihilism as the great danger of an age that had killed God yet kept the moral habits of religion. His project was to overcome nihilism — to affirm life in all its terror and splendor without recourse to comforting illusions.

His sister later weaponized fragments of his work for nationalist ends he would have loathed. Recovering the real Nietzsche means reading the lightning, not the lightning rod.

Great Ideas
  • God is dead: Not a celebration but a diagnosis — the collapse of absolute values leaves us adrift, and we must create meaning anew.
  • The Will to Power: Beneath the struggle for survival lies a deeper drive to expand, create, and overcome oneself.
  • The Übermensch (Overhuman): The one who authors their own values and affirms life, becoming who they are.
  • Eternal Recurrence: Live so that you would will to repeat this life, infinitely, exactly as it is.
  • Master & Slave Morality: A critique of morality as either a noble self-affirmation or a resentful inversion born of weakness.

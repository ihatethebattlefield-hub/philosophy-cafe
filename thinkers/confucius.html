Confucius

551 – 479 BCE · Lu (China)
Confucianism (Ru)
Biography

Kong Fuzi — "Master Kong" — was a teacher, editor, and wandering counselor in the turbulent Spring and Autumn period of China. Born to a poor but noble family, he devoted himself to learning and to the dream of a harmonious society restored through virtue and ritual.

He gathered disciples who recorded his sayings in the Analects — a slender book that shaped the civilization of East Asia for over two millennia. Though he never held the high office he sought, his influence as "the First Teacher" outlasted every emperor.

His philosophy is practical and relational: it begins not with abstract metaphysics but with how we ought to treat the person in front of us — a parent, a friend, a ruler.

Great Ideas
  • Ren (Humaneness): The supreme virtue of caring, often summed as "do not impose on others what you would not wish for yourself."
  • Li (Ritual & Propriety): Harmony is cultivated through respectful forms that shape the heart from the outside in.
  • Junzi (Exemplary Person): The moral ideal is not a sage born perfect but a person of cultivated character.
  • Filial Piety: The root of virtue lies in reverence for parents and ancestors, extending outward to society.
  • The Rectification of Names: Let words match reality — a ruler must rule, a father father — or disorder follows.
In His Words
"What you do not wish for yourself, do not do to others." — Confucius, Analects
"It does not matter how slowly you go as long as you do not stop." — Confucius

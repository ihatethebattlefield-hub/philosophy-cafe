Augustine of Hippo

354 – 430 CE · Thagaste & Hippo
Christian Philosophy
Biography

Augine was born in Roman North Africa to a Christian mother and a pagan father. He studied rhetoric in Carthage, taught in Rome and Milan, and pursued worldly ambition and Manichaean religion before a dramatic conversion in a Milan garden led him to Christianity.

His "Confessions" is the first great autobiography in Western literature — a searching account of memory, desire, sin, and grace. As bishop of Hippo he defended the church against heresies, wrote the monumental "City of God," and shaped Christian theology for centuries.

He fused biblical faith with Platonic philosophy, probing the nature of time, the will, and the inner life. His influence extends through medieval scholasticism, the Reformation, and modern psychology.

Great Ideas
  • The Restless Heart: "Our hearts are restless until they rest in Thee." Human desire is infinite and finds peace only in God.
  • Original Sin & Grace: Humanity is wounded by sin and redeemed by grace, not by merit alone — a doctrine that reshaped Western ethics.
  • Free Will & Providence: God foreknows all choices without causing them; human freedom and divine sovereignty remain in tension.
  • Time as Distention: Past and future exist only in the present mind. Time is the soul's stretching toward what is no longer and what is not yet.
  • The Two Cities: The City of God and the earthly city are interwoven in history, destined for separation at the end of time.

The Existential Prompt

Begin from "existence precedes essence"
Prompt · Free write
The Premise

A paper-cutter is made with a purpose, and so its essence precedes its existence. But a human being, Sartre argues, exists first — and only later, through choice, becomes anything at all. There is no human nature waiting to be discovered. There is only the terrible, glorious freedom to invent yourself.

Tonight, write as though no one is watching and nothing is fixed. Defend the person you choose to be.

The Exercise
  • Name a role you did not choose — a label handed to you by family, nation, or fate. Write it down and set it aside.
  • Now choose. If you were starting from a blank slate this morning, what would you will yourself to become?
  • Own the anxiety. Sartre calls it "anguish" — the dizziness of freedom. Describe the moment you feel it, and do not look away.
  • Act in bad faith? Recall a time you pretended you "had no choice." What were you protecting yourself from?
A Line to Carry
"Man is condemned to be free; because once thrown into the world, he is responsible for everything he does." — Sartre, Existentialism is a Humanism

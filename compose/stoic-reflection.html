The Stoic Reflection

An evening journal in the manner of Marcus Aurelius
Journal · 15 minutes
The Invitation

Light a candle, or simply close the door of the day behind you. The Stoic evening review is an ancient practice: a few quiet questions asked before sleep, by which a person becomes the author of their own character.

Marcus Aurelius wrote to himself, not to posterity. Treat this page the same way — honest, unhurried, free of performance.

Three Questions
  • What is within my control? Name the things that happened today which were truly yours to direct — your judgments, your responses, your efforts.
  • What is not? Name what belonged to fortune, to others, to the body. Did you mistake the two? Where did you give away your peace?
  • What would the wise do tomorrow? Choose one small habit to practice — a word withheld, a kindness offered, a fear faced.
A Line to Carry
"You have power over your mind — not outside events. Realize this, and you will find strength." — Marcus Aurelius, Meditations

The Metaphysics Meditation

Descartes' method of systematic doubt
Meditation · 20 minutes
The Premise

Descartes, seeking certainty, resolved to doubt everything that could possibly be doubted. The senses deceive. Dreams mimic waking life. Perhaps a malicious demon distorts even logic. Strip it all away — and ask what, if anything, survives.

This is not cynicism. It is the search for a foundation that no storm can wash away.

The Exercise
  • Doubt the senses. What have you been wrong about before? List three things you once "knew" with certainty and later revised.
  • Doubt the dream. How do you prove, right now, that you are awake? Write the best argument you can — then try to defeat it.
  • Find the indubitable. Descartes lands on the cogito: "I think, therefore I am." Can you doubt that you are doubting? What remains?
  • Rebuild. From that one point, what can you trust again, and on what grounds?
A Line to Carry
"I think, therefore I am." — René Descartes, Discourse on the Method
